Ingenous rocks are "Fire-Born", isn't that kinda cool?. Means they are formed from the cooling and solidification of molten(melted) rock. The word "igneous" derives from ignis, the latin word for fire. Molten rock material is known as magma until it is erupted onto the surface when it then is termed lava.

There are two broad types of igneous rocks:

  • Intrusive (plutonic) rocks
  • Extrusive (volcanic) rocks

Intrusive igneous rocks solidify within Earth. These rocks are also known as plutonic rocks—named for Pluto, the Roman god of the underworld. Intrusive igneous rocks are generally wholly crystalline and characterized by large crystal sizes visible to the naked eye because they cool slowly.

Extrusive igneous rocksre erupted onto the surface or into the atmosphere. Extrusive igneous rocks are also termed volcanic rocks—named for Vulcan, the Roman god of fire. Volcanic rocks have generally smaller crystal size and usually have a very-fine-grained or glassy groundmass that formed due to rapid cooling at the time of eruption. Volcanic rocks are erupted as lava flows or lava domes during nonexplosive (effusive) eruptions or as shattered bits of rock (volcanic ash, tephra, and volcanic bombs which are collectively called pyroclasts) during explosive eruptions. Pyroclastic rocks are made of volcanic ash and other pieces of pulverized volcanic rock. The term pyroclastic means “fire-broken” from “pryo-” (fire) and “-clastic” (broken) in Greek. Pyroclastic rocks include ash-fall and tephra-fall deposits as well as ash-flow tuffs (ignimbrites) that form from pyroclastic density currents.

THE ROCK!!!

Dwayne "The Rock" Johnson's tenure in WWE stands as one of the most electrifying and transformative periods in the history of professional wrestling. From his debut in 1996 as Rocky Maivia, the third-generation wrestler, to his eventual reinvention as "The Rock"—the brash, confident, and charismatic figure who became a global icon—his journey was nothing short of legendary. The Rock's rise was fueled by his natural athleticism, magnetic personality, and unparalleled mic skills. His ability to connect with fans through unforgettable catchphrases like “If ya smell what The Rock is cookin’” and his signature moves like "The People’s Elbow" quickly cemented his place as one of the most entertaining and beloved Superstars of all time. Whether as a cocky heel or a fan favorite, The Rock's words were just as powerful as his in-ring abilities, and his electrifying promos became a trademark of his career.

His iconic catchphrases, such as "Know Your Role" and "Can You Smell What The Rock Is Cookin'?" became synonymous with his larger-than-life persona. His feuds with legendary wrestlers like Stone Cold Steve Austin and Triple H were legendary, drawing millions of viewers and shaping the Attitude Era. The Rock's ability to connect with the crowd, whether as a beloved hero or a despised villain, was unmatched. He could effortlessly command the audience's attention, eliciting cheers and boos with equal fervor.

As a key figure during the Attitude Era, The Rock's rivalries with other WWE legends helped shape the landscape of professional wrestling. His iconic feuds with "Stone Cold" Steve Austin, Triple H, and Vince McMahon were pivotal to the success of WWE during the late '90s and early 2000s. His bouts with Austin, including their legendary clashes at WrestleMania 15, 17, and 19, are regarded as some of the best in WWE history, with each match adding another layer to the mythology of the Attitude Era. The Rock's ability to seamlessly transition between the roles of villain and hero, while maintaining his larger-than-life persona, kept fans engaged and on the edge of their seats. His charisma, athleticism, and storytelling in the ring made every match an event, while his constant ability to entertain through his promos solidified him as one of the greatest WWE Superstars of all time.

The Rock’s impact on WWE is immeasurable, both in terms of championships and cultural influence. He became a multi-time WWE Champion, a two-time Intercontinental Champion, a five-time Tag Team Champion, and won the Royal Rumble, marking his dominance in the company. His WrestleMania moments are legendary, particularly his historic battles with John Cena, which symbolized the passing of the torch to a new generation of WWE talent. Even after transitioning to a Hollywood career, The Rock remained a key figure in WWE, returning for high-profile appearances that reminded fans of his roots. His legacy in WWE isn’t just measured by titles or matches, but by his enduring impact on the business and culture of professional wrestling. The Rock helped elevate WWE to new heights of popularity, becoming a global megastar whose influence extends far beyond the squared circle. His contributions to WWE and professional wrestling will be remembered as a defining chapter in the history of the sport.
